The 10 Best Cars to Buy Now in March 2021

Audi Q5

44,300

The Audi Q5 is one of the top-rated models in our ranking of luxury compact SUVs, meaning it should be on the radar of anyone shopping in this segment. It’s a well-built vehicle inside and out, with strong safety ratings from both the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) and the Insurance Institute for Highway Safety (IIHS). Like many Audi vehicles, the infotainment tech inside the Q5 is among the best in the segment, as it is easy to use and rich with features. All Q5s come equipped with Audi’s Quattro all-wheel-drive system, so the Q5 should keep you moving in poor weather.

Honda Accord

24,770

Shoppers looking for the classic sedan have excellent options to choose from these days, despite the shift in demand towards crossovers. The Honda Accord is a top-notch example that places at the top of our midsize cars ranking, thanks to a wide roster of family-friendly qualities. It boasts ample interior space for people and cargo, and the seats are comfortable for both short and long journeys. Infotainment and safety tech is abundant as well. The Accord is an excellent value, earning a finalist position for our 2021 Best Midsize Car for the Money award.

Kia Soul

17,590

The Kia Soul is a top-notch crossover, and has appropriately earned a number of accolades over the years. This year, it won our 2021 Best Subcompact SUV for the Money award, given its unrivaled value in the subcompact SUV segment. For the price, you’re getting loads of passenger and cargo space despite a small footprint, as well as user-friendly infotainment tech. Its quirky styling means you’ll be driving something that doesn’t look like many other vehicles on the road.

BMW X1

35,400

Luxury subcompact SUVs are popular for good reason, and the BMW X1 is currently our top-ranked option in the class. If you’re looking for a peppy crossover with luxury appointments, the X1 is worth a close look. Behind the wheel, drivers will enjoy sporty handling and a capable four-cylinder engine. Behind the back seat, the large cargo hold is competitive with its rivals like the Audi Q3 and Mercedes-Benz GLA. Combining fun and practicality, the X1 is a great choice for daily drivers and weekend warriors alike.

Audi A4

39,100

The Audi A4 nails the luxury sedan formula, securing it a spot among the top three in our ranking of luxury small cars. The interior is comfortable and well-appointed with luxury materials, while the infotainment technology is feature-laden while remaining easy to use. Its comfortable yet composed ride quality also underpins the daily luxury experience in the A4.

Mercedes-Benz E-Class

54,250

If you’re looking for a car filled with opulence, consider the 2021 Mercedes-Benz E-Class. The refreshed E-Class is a high-scoring and well-rounded sedan in our ranking of luxury midsize cars and it comes equipped with tons of amenities. For starters, it has an upscale cabin with a plethora of features and premium materials. It also offers four powerful engine options that are capable of providing speedy acceleration.

Toyota Avalon

35,875

Shoppers in the market for a large car should consider the dependable Toyota Avalon, which places near the top of our ranking of full-size sedans. In addition to its above-average predicted reliability rating, the Avalon has a robust V6 engine and a comfortable ride. Its interior comes with some of the nicest materials in its class, and higher trims provide a luxurious atmosphere. There is plenty of room inside the cabin for passengers to stretch out, plus numerous standard features that are easy to use. In addition, the Avalon won our 2021 Best Large Car for the Money award because of its excellent combination of quality and value.

Mazda3

20,500

If you’re looking for a sporty compact car, check out the 2021 Mazda3. It currently holds the top spot in the compact car class and is available in both sedan and hatchback forms. Its new-for-2021 base 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine plenty of power for daily driving, while both of its optional engines deliver even more punch. Its suspension lets you tackle corners with ease while still providing a comfortable ride.

Nissan Rogue

25,650

For those interested in a compact SUV, be sure to check out the fully redesigned 2021 Nissan Rogue. This crossover places near the top of our ranking of compact SUVs and comes with a premium cabin and plenty of interior and cargo space. The Rogue handles well and is fuel-efficient, getting an EPA-estimated 27 mpg in the city and 35 mpg on the highway. Its predicted reliability score is also higher than most.

Toyota Sienna

34,460

If you’re looking for an efficient minivan for your family, make sure to check out the 2021 Toyota Sienna. Following a full redesign this year, it’s now only available with a hybrid powertrain, giving it excellent fuel economy. The Sienna has responsive steering and handles well for a minivan. Inside, you’ll find an upscale interior with plenty of room for passengers.
